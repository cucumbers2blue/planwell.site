Task A: Time In Music Notation

Back to Grade 6Open Blackhole

Unit Direction

In this unit, you will investigate how modern music notation helps musicians communicate time.

History will be used as a short starter: people needed notation because music had to be remembered, shared, and coordinated. The main focus of your Task A is modern notation: pulse, bars, time signatures, note values, and rests.

This task will help you understand rhythm before later tasks where you will perform or create rhythms.

Statement Of Inquiry

Musicians invented notation to communicate musical time clearly.

Criteria Assessed

MYP Arts Criterion A: Investigating Stage: Year 1 / Novice

  • Strand i: Investigate a musical context related to the statement of inquiry.
  • Strand ii: Describe an artwork or performance from the chosen context.

Task Overview

You will create:

  1. A hand-drawn A3 paper infographic explaining the principles of time in modern music notation. This shows your theory understanding.
  2. An annotated music sheet showing how notation communicates time in one short rhythm example. This shows your practical understanding.

Required Evidence

1. Infographic

Your infographic should explain all of these:

  • pulse / beat
  • barlines and bars
  • time signature
  • note values
  • rests
  • how musicians count time
  • how notation helps musicians play together

Use clear headings, colours, symbols, drawings, and short explanations. Your infographic should be as detailed as it needs to be to cover the list above clearly.

2. Annotated Music Sheet

Find one short rhythm example or music sheet to annotate. Ask the teacher to help or approve your choice if you are unsure.

Your annotation should identify:

  • time signature
  • barlines
  • beats
  • note values
  • rests
  • how the notation shows when sound happens and when silence happens

Each annotation should have a label and a short sentence. Do not only circle symbols.

Helpful Vocabulary

  • Annotate: add labels and short notes to explain what you see.
  • Pulse / beat: the steady count in the music.
  • Bar: a small group of beats.
  • Barline: the line that separates bars.
  • Time signature: the numbers that show how beats are grouped.
  • Note value: how long a note lasts.
  • Rest: a written silence.

Task-Specific Clarification: Criterion A

To achieve well in Criterion A, you should:

  • explain how modern notation shows musical time
  • use correct music vocabulary
  • organize information clearly in your infographic
  • annotate the music sheet accurately
  • describe how the example shows beat, note length, silence, and bars
  • use your own words to show your understanding

Differentiation

All students complete the same Criterion A goal: investigate how notation communicates time and annotate one musical example. Students may use approved supports such as vocabulary lists, sentence starters, annotation guides, symbol banks, and staged checkpoints.

Differentiation is provided through content, process, product, language support, learning needs, and extension. The assessment criterion is not changed; access, scaffolding, complexity, and independence are adjusted according to student readiness and learning needs.

Support Options

  • time-symbol bank
  • partially annotated rhythm sheet
  • infographic layout options
  • sentence starters
  • teacher conference during planning

Extension Options

  • compare 2/4, 3/4, and 4/4
  • include ties or different rest values
  • create and annotate an original rhythm
  • explain why time signatures and barlines help musicians coordinate

Academic Integrity

Your words, labels, and drawings should show your own understanding. Do not copy full sentences from websites or other students.

Submission Checklist
